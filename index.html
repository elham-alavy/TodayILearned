WHAT IS APACHE SPARK — AND WHY IT CHANGED EVERYTHING
(\(\ ( -.-) < "spark goes brrr 🚀" o_(")(") 🐰 khargosh says:
Apache Spark is a distributed data processing engine — it splits your data across many computers and processes them all at once. Before Spark, processing huge datasets took hours. Spark does it in minutes by keeping data in memory instead of writing to disk every step. For public health data — think millions of patient records — this is a game changer.
> "Today I learned that Spark doesn't just process data faster — it changes what questions you can even afford to ask."

Relationships & Boundaries — what no one tells you 💙
(\(\ (-.-) < "protect your peace..." o_(")(") 🐻 drawing the line
"Some relationships become the slap you never needed."

Human relationships are complicated. We often think that patience and giving more love will solve everything — at work, with friends. But sometimes we fall into the illusion that if we come closer, love more, overlook more... they will change.

The reality is that our time is our most valuable asset. Trying to fix someone who doesn't want to change only exhausts us — it wears down our soul.

Unlike the cliché "every person teaches us a lesson" — not every experience needs to be a lesson. Some people are simply toxic and leave poisonous traces on our mind.

Transparency is a skill of maturity. But it shouldn't be a blank check — because a relationship means mutual interaction. It means staying and growing together — not just tolerating and leaving.

Go toward the spaces and people who value your presence and effort.
"Today I learned that not every door that closes needs to be reopened — some are meant to stay shut."
